Frequently Asked Questions

How are these guides different from generic advice?

Unlike generic advice that tells you to "just stop" or rely on willpower, our guides are based on the neuroscience of addiction and habit formation. We identify the specific psychological hooks and neurological mechanisms that make each behavior addictive, then provide targeted interventions to disrupt those patterns at their root.

Do I need to quit completely or can I cut back?

Both approaches are valid and covered in our guides. Some people benefit from complete abstinence, especially with highly addictive substances or behaviors. Others find success with controlled reduction or harm reduction approaches. The guides help you determine what approach works best for your specific situation and goals.

How long does it take to see results?

Most people notice reductions in cravings and compulsive urges within 1-2 weeks of implementing the strategies. Significant habit changes typically occur within 4-8 weeks. Lasting neural rewiring and establishment of new default behaviors usually takes 3-6 months of consistent practice.

Is this approach based on scientific research?

Yes. Every strategy in our guides is based on peer-reviewed research from neuroscience, psychology, and behavioral science. We draw from established theories of habit formation, addiction neurobiology, cognitive behavioral therapy, and mindfulness-based interventions. All recommendations are evidence-based and clinically informed.
